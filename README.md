# Smart Tangibles: Strategic Product Management for Connected Hardware

A comprehensive business book guiding hardware entrepreneurs and product managers through the transformation of physical products into software-augmented platforms.

## About the Book

Physical products are becoming software platforms. Hardware companies face margin compression, commoditization, and competition from software-first players. **Smart Tangibles** provides the frameworks, case studies, and practical guidance needed to navigate this transformation.

The book draws on real-world examples from companies including Apple, Tesla, BYD, iRobot, Swatch, and others to illustrate how hardware products can build defensible competitive positions through digital capabilities.

## Book Structure

The book is organized into **6 arcs** spanning **26 chapters**:

| Arc | Title | Chapters | Focus |
|-----|-------|----------|-------|
| I | Hardware Challenges, Old and New | 0-4 | Why hardware is hard, validation traps, platform absorption, evolving competition |
| II | Infrastructure Foundations | 5-9 | Digital interfaces, sensing, connectivity, firmware updates |
| III | Validation Cycles | 10-13 | Taming concepts, prototyping beyond mockups, the new MVP |
| IV | Positioning & Perceptions | 14-18 | First impressions, killer features, the Swatch Moment |
| V | Paradise Lost | 19-21 | Security, cyber threats, societal impact |
| VI | Business & Moats | 22-25 | Business models, recurring revenue, Product Valhalla |

## Who Should Read This Book

- Product managers transitioning to hardware companies
- Hardware startup founders and entrepreneurs
- Innovation leaders in established hardware companies
- Engineers bridging physical and digital product development
